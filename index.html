The Future of Education: The Impact We Are Living Or Not

By: Sanket Gollakota — August 2017

Many people still find educational institutions such as schools, colleges/universities, vocational institutes etc. important because they are the basis of living. They are the main source of socialization, as these institutions provide students with a stable environment to study and to build strong personal qualities. In the 21st century, though, many educational institutions seem to be in a way obsolete for the real world. There are several reasons why the concept of learning education in the 21st Century is outdated.

As an educator, it is important to remember that there are a few important factors that are crucial to a successful educational process. The major key that is needed to have a successful learning experience, according to our teacher trainer, is a willingness to be an active and active member of a group. The key to success with a group of students is to develop a relationship with them that does not limit them from progressing at their own pace but instead moves faster than they can. I find this concept to be extremely important in learning for the 21st Century. Students need to feel respected, appreciated and valued. To do this, to work through an academic experience for the 21st Century, students need to be actively engaged and involved with their learning and with their teachers. We don’t want a disengaged or unengaged student.

I find it really helps to consider who your learners are, and then come up with a curriculum or syllabus to meet their learning needs. This gives you time to reflect on what they want to learn, then choose curriculum materials which will ensure they are engaged and active learners. Inquiry processes occur when a child can observe what students around them are doing in order to decide what should be done next. In other words, questions should occur to the child to ask.
